\begin{abstract}
Energy-based models (EBMs) offer a natural route to controllable generation in discrete domains by composing objectives and constraints directly into a scalar energy. However, EBMs for graphs often assign unreliable off-distribution energies, which degrades sampling and leads to a sizable fidelity gap relative to discrete diffusion models. We introduce Graph Energy Matching (GEM), a generative framework for graphs that closes this gap. Motivated by a discrete Jordan–Kinderlehrer–Otto (JKO) perspective, GEM learns a single permutation-invariant potential that simultaneously provides transport-aligned guidance from a simple source distribution toward data-supported regions, and likelihood-based refinement within these high-probability regions. GEM samples using an energy-based switch between a transport phase with gradient-informed edits to rapidly reach high-probability configurations and a Metropolis–Hastings mixing phase with local, geometry-aware proposals. On MOSES and QM9, GEM achieves unconditional generation quality on par with, and in some settings exceeding, state-of-the-art discrete diffusion models. Beyond sample quality, the learned potential provides a direct mechanism for test-time constraint composition by augmenting the energy, and its explicit relative-likelihood structure enables additional capabilities such as computing geodesics—minimum-energy edit paths aligned with the learned data geometry.
\end{abstract}
