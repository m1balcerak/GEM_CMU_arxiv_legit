\begin{abstract}
%Energy-based models (EBMs) model rel likelihood explicitly which enables probabilistic inference - exciting things at inference ..... (beyond control, control is e.g.)

Energy-based models (EBMs) offer a natural route to controllable generation in discrete domains (e.g., graphs) by composing objectives and constraints directly into a scalar energy. However, EBMs for graphs often assign unreliable off-distribution energies, which degrades sampling and leads to a sizable fidelity gap relative to discrete diffusion models. We introduce Graph Energy Matching (GEM), a generative framework for graphs that closes this gap. Motivated by a discrete Jordan–Kinderlehrer–Otto (JKO) perspective, GEM learns a permutation-invariant potential that simultaneously provides transport-aligned guidance from a noise to data, and likelihood-based refinement within high-probability regions. GEM samples using an energy-based switch between a transport phase with gradient-informed edits to rapidly reach high-probability configurations and a Metropolis–Hastings mixing phase with local, geometry-aware proposals. On molecular graph benchmarks (MOSES, QM9), GEM matches or exceeds strong discrete diffusion baselines. Beyond sample quality alone, GEM's learned potential naturally enables property-constrained generation at test-time, and its explicit relative-likelihood structure further supports analyses such as computing geodesics—minimum-energy edit paths aligned with the learned data geometry.
\end{abstract}
