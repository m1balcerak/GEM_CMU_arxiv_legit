\begin{abstract}
We propose Graph Energy Matching (\gls{gem}), an energy-based controllable generative framework for discrete spaces,
with a particular focus on graphs. \gls{gem} learns a scalar \emph{potential energy} $V_\theta$ that
(i) transports a simple reference distribution using symmetry-aware graph matchings and
(ii) refines the energy near data with a contrastive sampling stage. The learned energy is
graph-isomorphism invariant (invariant to node relabelings). The approach is theoretically grounded within the
Jordan--Kinderlehrer--Otto gradient-flow view for discrete spaces. A single scalar field captures data
likelihood structure while enabling geometry-aware discrete sampling. For sampling, \gls{gem} uses multi-site discrete
proposals: energy differences are linearized using $\nabla V_\theta$ and large edits are
suppressed by a locality penalty derived from the same graph cost used for matching, yielding
efficient exploration. We evaluate our approach on molecular graph generation in both unconditional
and challenging conditional settings targeting drug-like properties.\textcolor{red}{[Results are Work In Progress (WiP): unconditional FCD is SOTA; conditional results look SOTA, but we will report final metrics after a systematic evaluation.]}
\end{abstract}
