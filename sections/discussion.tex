\section{Discussion and Conclusion}
We introduced \emph{Graph Energy Matching} (\gls{gem}), a discrete generative modeling framework motivated by the map-optimization perspective of the \gls{jko} scheme, extending continuous energy matching principles to discrete graph spaces. \gls{gem} integrates deterministic, transport-aligned graph edits guiding sampling toward high-probability regions with principled \gls{mcmc} mixing under a unified scalar energy model. Conceptually, this allows (i) scalable and high-quality molecular graph generation from both noise- and data-initialized states, and (ii) flexible conditional generation at inference-time through an explicitly learned relative-likelihood structure. Empirically, \gls{gem} achieves state-of-the-art performance among graph \glspl{ebm}, matching or surpassing diffusion and \gls{ctmc}-based baselines on MOSES and QM9 datasets, and effectively addresses property optimization under threshold constraints. Moreover, its explicit energy parameterization uniquely facilitates downstream diagnostic analyses, including direct likelihood comparisons, controllability insights, and precise geodesic computations—tasks inherently challenging for implicit generative approaches.


\paragraph{Limitations and Trade-offs.}  \gls{gem} explicitly learns a scalar energy field capturing relative likelihood; thus, like other \glspl{ebm}, generation velocities must be derived from gradients, incurring a $\sim$2--3$\times$ theoretical overhead compared to purely forward parameterizations. Nonetheless, this explicit representation naturally supports challenging tasks like compositional inference and computing geometry-aware geodesics.


\paragraph{Future Directions.}
\gls{gem} could be extended to joint 2D and 3D molecular modeling~\citep{liu2022pre,chen2023molecule} by explicitly encoding geometric constraints, such as interatomic distances, into its robust graph prior. Furthermore, the discrete proposals underlying \gls{gem} provide a natural foundation for broader discrete domains, including text and token-based modeling, where energy-based perspectives are increasingly prominent~\citep{blondel2025autoregressive,xu2025energy}.
