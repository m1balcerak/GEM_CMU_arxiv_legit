\section{Experiments}
\label{sec:experiments}

We report unconditional generation on MOSES and QM9, conditional generation/property optimization on MOSES as well as geodesic comparisons based on the learned potential. MOSES provides a large-scale benchmark (about 1.5M molecules), while QM9 is smaller; we use MOSES to show both the efficacy and scalability of GEM and QM9 as an additional compact controlled comparison. For all experiments, we report
\gls{vun} and \gls{fcd} (lower is better), and in general consider two initialization regimes: noise initialization
starts from easy-to-sample noise and must reach chemically valid molecules, while data initialization
starts from training molecules and must make novel yet valid edits (diffusion/\gls{ctmc} or \gls{mcmc}).
%
A concise conceptual summary of the baselines used in our experiments is provided in
Appendix~\ref{app:baselines}, and the experimental setup is detailed in Appendix~\ref{app:exp-setup}.

\subsection*{Conditional generation}
%\paragraph{Property Optimization.}
We conduct property optimization with regards to a diverse set of properties including the logarithm of lipophilicity (logP), the logarithm of aqueous solubility (logS), \gls{qed}, \gls{tpsa}. For comparability, we closely follow  ~\citep{ninniri2025graph}: we train a GraphTransformer regressor \(f_\phi\) for
differentiable guidance and define \(V_\theta^{\mathrm{cond}}(x)=V_\theta(x)+\lambda_{\mathrm{prop}}\|f_\phi(x)-y^\star\|^2\),
then run the same proposal kernel $q_\theta(x\!\to\!y)$ using \(-\nabla_x V_\theta^{\mathrm{cond}}(x)\),
with the \gls{mh} acceptance ratio modified accordingly. For fairness, all methods use
noise-conditioned regressors (\gls{gem} clean-regressor ablation are in Appendix~\ref{app:regressor-noise},
Figure~\ref{fig:regressor-noise}, Table~\ref{tab:regressor-ablations}) and we feed regressor time via
an energy-based proxy that increases linearly until the chain reaches the data distribution, then
clamps to $t=1$ (Appendix~\ref{app:regressor-noise}). Noise-conditioned regressors are more reliable
on noisy or invalid graphs, and targets (molecular threshold constraints: logS $\ge$ -2.25, logP $\le$ 1.5, \gls{qed} $\ge$ 0.9,
\gls{tpsa} $\le$ 50) are chosen such that unconditional MOSES samples satisfy the constraint at roughly a 20\%
rate, yielding comparable difficulty across properties.
Table~\ref{tab:moses-conditional} reports the results:
\gls{gem} maintains strong validity, uniqueness, and novelty while improving constraint satisfaction relative to baselines.

\paragraph{Geodesic Analysis}
Energy-based models have been used to study shortest paths in high-dimensional spaces \cite{bethune2025follow}. Here, we compute geodesics, minimum-energy paths through graph edits aligned with the learned data geometry, between molecules and compare against a linear interpolation baseline (cost-only). 
We plot average validity versus the endpoint distance induced by the \gls{fgw} matching cost
$c_{\mathrm{FGW}}$ (Appendix~\ref{app:matching}), see Figure~\ref{fig:geodesic-validity}. Qualitative paths are shown in
Figure~\ref{fig:mol-trajectories}.
Energy-weighted geodesics consistently preserve validity relative to cost-only geodesics, confirming
that the learned energy captures chemically meaningful geometry. Full protocol, pairing,
optimization, and discretization details are in Appendix~\ref{app:geodesic-details}.
