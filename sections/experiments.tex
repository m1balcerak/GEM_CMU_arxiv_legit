\section{Experiments}
\label{sec:experiments}

We report unconditional generation on MOSES and QM9, conditional generation/property optimization on MOSES, and geodesic comparisons based on the learned potential. MOSES provides a larger-scale benchmark ($\sim$1.5M molecules), demonstrating the scalability of GEM. QM9 is a widely used benchmark where performance is largely saturated. Differences in scores often reflect hyperparameter tuning rather than fundamental modeling capabilities; however, we include it for completeness and to demonstrate \gls{gem}'s competitiveness.

For all experiments, we report \gls{vun} (or \gls{vu}+\textit{Novelty} on QM9) and \gls{fcd}; for conditional tasks we report \gls{cvun}, considering two initialization regimes: noise initialization, where generation starts from easy-to-sample noise and must reach chemically valid molecules; and data initialization, applicable only to EBMs, which starts from training molecules and must produce novel molecules through edits.

\subsection{Conditional Generation}
%\paragraph{Property Optimization.}
We conduct property optimization with respect to a diverse set of properties including the logarithm of lipophilicity (logP), the logarithm of aqueous solubility (logS), \gls{qed}, \gls{tpsa}. For comparability, we follow  ~\citep{vignac2022digress, ninniri2025graph}: we train a GraphTransformer regressor \(f_\phi\) for
differentiable guidance and define \(V_\theta^{\mathrm{cond}}(x)=V_\theta(x)+\lambda_{\mathrm{prop}}\|f_\phi(x)-y^\star\|^2\),
then run the same proposal kernel $q_\theta(x\!\to\!y)$ using \(-\nabla_x V_\theta^{\mathrm{cond}}(x)\),
with the \gls{mh} acceptance ratio modified accordingly. The above scheme can also be modified to accommodate a time- or noise-conditioned property regressor. For fairness, all methods reported in \Cref{tab:moses-conditional} use noise-conditioned regressors (the \gls{gem} clean-regressor ablations are provided in \Cref{app:regressor-noise}, ~\Cref{fig:regressor-noise}, and ~\Cref{tab:regressor-ablations}). We feed regressor time using an energy-based proxy that increases linearly until the chain reaches the data distribution, after which it is clamped to $t=1$ (\Cref{app:regressor-noise}). Noise-conditioned regressors provide more reliable predictions on noisy or invalid graphs. The target molecular property constraints (logS $\ge$ -2.25, logP $\le$ 1.5, \gls{qed} $\ge$ 0.9, \gls{tpsa} $\le$ 50) are selected such that unconditional MOSES samples satisfy each constraint approximately 20\% of the time, ensuring comparable difficulty across properties.
 \Cref{tab:moses-conditional} reports the results:
\gls{gem} maintains strong validity, uniqueness, and novelty while improving constraint satisfaction relative to baselines.

\paragraph{Geodesic Analysis}
Energy-based models have been used to study shortest paths in high-dimensional spaces \cite{bethune2025follow}. Here, we compute geodesics, minimum-energy paths through graph edits aligned with the learned data geometry, between molecules and compare against a linear interpolation baseline (cost-only). 
We plot average validity versus the endpoint distance induced by the node-matching approximation to the cost
$c_{\mathrm{FGW}}$ (~\Cref{app:matching}), see ~\Cref{fig:geodesic-validity}. Qualitative paths are shown in
~\Cref{fig:mol-trajectories}.
Energy-weighted geodesics consistently preserve validity compared to cost-only geodesics, confirming that the learned energy encodes chemically meaningful geometry. Experimental details, including pairing, optimization, and discretization, are provided in ~\Cref{app:geodesic-details}.
