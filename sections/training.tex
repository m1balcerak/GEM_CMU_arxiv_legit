\subsection{Training objectives}
\label{sec:training}

\paragraph{Interpolation and Minibatch coupling.}
Given minibatches $x^0 \sim \pi_0$ and $x^\mathrm{data} \sim \pi_\mathrm{data}$, we match samples using a
permutation-invariant approximation to the \gls{fgw}-type graph cost $c(x^0,x^\mathrm{data})$
(Section~\ref{sec:gem} and Appendix~\ref{app:matching}), obtaining a pairing map $T$ induced by the
minibatch coupling. Each source graph $x^0$ is paired with a data graph $x^\mathrm{data}=T(x^0)$.
In all experiments involving our method, we initialize from a uniform noise distribution $\pi_0$, in contrast to diffusion-based baselines which typically utilize the marginal noise distribution.

Crucially, we utilize $c_{\mathrm{FGW}}$ solely to select optimal pairs based on global structural similarity (e.g., node and edge counts). Once paired, we rely on the isotropy of the uniform source: since the noise graph lacks intrinsic node ordering, any node permutation is statistically equivalent. We therefore treat the sampled node ordering of $x^0$ as implicitly aligned with the paired $x^{\mathrm{data}}$ via the identity permutation. This approach is compatible with our architecture because $V_\theta$ is permutation-invariant by construction (Appendix~\ref{app:perm-invariance}), ensuring the learned energy landscape is independent of the specific stochastic node labeling.
Denote their embeddings by $\hat{x}^0$ and $\hat{x}^\mathrm{data}$. Define the
displacement in the embedding space as $v=\hat{x}^\mathrm{data}-\hat{x}^0$.
We sample a \emph{discrete} interpolant $x^t:=(x^t_\mathcal{V},x^t_\mathcal{E})$, where $x_\mathcal{V}^t:= [x_i^t]_{i=1}^n$ and $x_\mathcal{E}^t := [x_{ij}^t]_{i<j}$ by independently sampling each node and edge from the endpoint graphs.
\begin{eqnarray}
x_i^t \sim \operatorname{Cat}\Big((1-t)\,\phi_\mathcal{V}\big(x_i^0\big) + t\,\phi_\mathcal{V}\big(x_i^{\mathrm{data}}\big)\Big)\\
x_{ij}^t \sim \operatorname{Cat}\Big((1-t)\,\phi_\mathcal{E}\big(x_{ij}^0\big) + t\,\phi_\mathcal{E}\big(x_{ij}^{\mathrm{data}}\big)\Big)
\end{eqnarray}
% \end{equation}
Let $\hat{x}^t$ be the continuous embedding of the discrete graph $x^t$ via $\phi_\mathcal{V},\phi_\mathcal{E}$ (Section~\ref{sec:gem}). While this embedding resides in a continuous ambient space, training is conducted solely on discrete graphs $x \in \mathcal{X}$, as both energy and sampling proposals are evaluated primarily through discrete jumps. The continuous embedding thus mainly facilitates gradient computation of $\nabla_{\hat{x}_t} V_\theta(\hat{x}^t)$ to guide these discrete transitions.



\paragraph{Objectives.}
We use the optimality condition derived in \Cref{proposals} to construct the Flow loss ($\mathcal{L}_{\text{Flow}}$). We set the JKO step-size parameter $\eta=1$. Consequently, the optimality condition $\nabla_x V(x) = -\nabla_x c(x,y)$ implies that the negative energy gradient should match the displacement vector $v$ pointing toward the data. While the pairing is determined by global signatures, the local training objective minimizes the Euclidean cost ($c_{\text{loc}}$) along the linear interpolation path:
\begin{align}
\mathcal{L}_{\text{Flow}}(\theta)
&=
\mathbb{E}_{(x^0,x^\mathrm{data})\sim\tilde{\gamma},\,t\sim\Unif([0,1])}\left[
\|\nabla_{\hat{x}_t} V_\theta(\hat{x}^t)+v\|_2^2
\right],\\
\mathcal{L}_{\text{CL}}(\theta)
&=
\mathbb{E}_{x^{+}\sim \pi_\mathrm{data}}[V_\theta(\hat{x}^{+})]
-\mathbb{E}_{x^{-}\sim \text{sg}\,\pi_\theta}[V_\theta(\hat{x}^{-})].
\end{align} 
The loss $\mathcal{L}_{\text{CL}}$ remains as defined in \Cref{eq:cl}, except that the sampling method employs the discrete proposals introduced in \Cref{proposals_lang}, rather than the continuous SDE from \Cref{em_sde}. Note that we supervise the model solely at discrete graph candidates, as the proposals require evaluation of energies and gradients only at discrete graph states.

Relaxations of the transport objective near $t=1$ are discussed in Appendix~\ref{app:flow-weighting}.
We minimize
\begin{equation}
\min_\theta\;
\mathcal{L}_{\text{Flow}}(\theta)
+\lambda_{\text{CL}}\,
\mathcal{L}_{\text{CL}}(\theta).
\end{equation}
We use a warm-up of $N_{\mathrm{warmup}}$ iterations with only $\mathcal{L}_{\text{Flow}}$ before
turning on $\mathcal{L}_{\text{CL}}$, following the continuous formulation~\citep{balcerak2025energy};
the same intuition carries over to the discrete case by yielding better negatives. See \Cref{app:exp-setup} for specific hyperparameters.
