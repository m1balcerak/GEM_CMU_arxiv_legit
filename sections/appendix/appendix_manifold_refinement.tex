\section{Contrastive loss objective derivation}\label{app:energy-refinement}

Let the model density be
\begin{equation}
p_\theta(x)=\frac{e^{-E_\theta(x)}}{Z_\theta},
\qquad
Z_\theta=\int e^{-E_\theta(x)}\,\mathrm{d}x.
\end{equation}
Maximum likelihood estimation solves
\begin{equation}
\theta^\star=\arg\max_\theta\;\mathbb{E}_{x\sim p_{\text{data}}}\big[\log p_\theta(x)\big]
\;\;\Longleftrightarrow\;\;
\theta^\star=\arg\min_\theta\;\mathcal{J}(\theta),
\end{equation}
with
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{J}(\theta):=-\mathbb{E}_{p_{\text{data}}}\big[\log p_\theta(x)\big]
=\mathbb{E}_{p_{\text{data}}}\big[E_\theta(x)\big]+\log Z_\theta.
\end{equation}
Taking gradients gives
\begin{equation}
\nabla_\theta \mathcal{J}(\theta)
=\mathbb{E}_{p_{\text{data}}}\big[\nabla_\theta E_\theta(x)\big]
+\nabla_\theta \log Z_\theta,
\end{equation}
and
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
\nabla_\theta \log Z_\theta
&=\frac{1}{Z_\theta}\nabla_\theta Z_\theta
=\frac{1}{Z_\theta}\int e^{-E_\theta(x)}\big(-\nabla_\theta E_\theta(x)\big)\,\mathrm{d}x\\
&=-\mathbb{E}_{p_\theta}\big[\nabla_\theta E_\theta(x)\big].
\end{aligned}
\end{equation}
Therefore,
\begin{equation}
\boxed{
\nabla_\theta \mathcal{J}(\theta)
=\mathbb{E}_{p_{\text{data}}}\big[\nabla_\theta E_\theta(x)\big]
-\mathbb{E}_{p_\theta}\big[\nabla_\theta E_\theta(x)\big]
}.
\end{equation}
In our setting, \(E_\theta(x)=\beta_{mh} V_\theta(x)\), and we approximate the model expectation with samples from the current chain while stopping gradients through the sampler, yielding the energy refinement objective in the main text.
