\section{Conceptualizing baselines}
\label{app:baselines}

We briefly summarize the baseline methods referenced in the experiments to clarify the modeling
assumptions behind each comparison.

\paragraph{GraphEBM.}
GraphEBM~\citep{liu2021graphebm} learns a scalar energy over molecular graphs with a GNN and relies on
MCMC (e.g., Metropolis-Hastings over local graph edits) for sampling, without an explicit transport
or flow-matching objective.

\paragraph{DeFoG.}
DeFoG~\citep{qinmadeira2024defog} formulates graph generation as discrete flow matching, learning
CTMC transition rates that connect source and data distributions; sampling follows the learned
continuous-time dynamics.

\paragraph{VFM.}
Variational Flow Matching (VFM)~\citep{eijkelboom2024vfm} reframes flow matching as variational
inference over trajectory endpoints, yielding globally consistent objectives for discrete graph
generation via CTMC paths.

\paragraph{G-VFM.}
G-VFM~\citep{eijkelboom2025controlledvfm} extends VFM with equivariant conditioning mechanisms to
support controllable generation, typically by incorporating property guidance into the flow-matching
framework.
