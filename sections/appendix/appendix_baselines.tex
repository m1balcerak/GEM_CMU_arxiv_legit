\section{Conceptualizing baselines todo}
\label{app:baselines}

We summarize the baseline methods used in our experiments with an emphasis on the generation/samplign mechanismn for each method. 

\paragraph{GraphEBM.}
GraphEBM~\citep{liu2021graphebm} models molecular graphs using an energy-based model that assigns a scalar energy $E_\theta(G)$ to each graph $G$ via a graph neural network.
Unlike transport- or flow-based approaches, GraphEBM does not learn an explicit transformation from a source distribution to the data distribution. Instead, generation relies on Langevin dynamics sampling.

%or flow-matching objective.

\paragraph{DeFoG.}
DeFoG~\citep{qinmadeira2024defog} formulates discrete graph generation as (continuous-time) discrete
flow matching. It learns a continuous-time Markov chain (CTMC) whose transition rates are trained to connect a simple source distribution to the data distribution along a time-indexed stochastic path.
Sampling proceeds by simulating the learned CTMC dynamics from the source to obtain a final graph.

%continuous-time dynamics.

\paragraph{\gls{vfm}.}
\gls{vfm}~\citep{eijkelboom2024vfm} reframes flow matching as variational
inference over trajectory endpoints, yielding globally consistent objectives for discrete graph
generation via \gls{ctmc} paths.

\paragraph{G-\gls{vfm}.}
G-\gls{vfm}~\citep{eijkelboom2025controlledvfm} extends \gls{vfm} with equivariant conditioning mechanisms to
support controllable generation, typically by incorporating property guidance into the flow-matching
framework.
