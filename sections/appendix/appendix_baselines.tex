\section{Conceptualizing baselines todo}
\label{app:baselines}

We briefly summarize the baseline methods referenced in the experiments to clarify the modeling
assumptions behind each comparison.

\paragraph{GraphEBM.}
GraphEBM~\citep{liu2021graphebm} learns a scalar energy over molecular graphs with a \gls{gnn} and relies on
\gls{mcmc} (e.g., \gls{mh} over local graph edits) for sampling, without an explicit transport
or flow-matching objective.

\paragraph{DeFoG.}
DeFoG~\citep{qinmadeira2024defog} formulates graph generation as discrete flow matching, learning
\gls{ctmc} transition rates that connect source and data distributions; sampling follows the learned
continuous-time dynamics.

\paragraph{\gls{vfm}.}
\gls{vfm}~\citep{eijkelboom2024vfm} reframes flow matching as variational
inference over trajectory endpoints, yielding globally consistent objectives for discrete graph
generation via \gls{ctmc} paths.

\paragraph{G-\gls{vfm}.}
G-\gls{vfm}~\citep{eijkelboom2025controlledvfm} extends \gls{vfm} with equivariant conditioning mechanisms to
support controllable generation, typically by incorporating property guidance into the flow-matching
framework.
